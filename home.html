Ingredients:

2 cups of mixed fruits (such as grapes, strawberries, pineapple, mandarin oranges, and kiwi),
chopped into bite-sized pieces
1 cup of miniature marshmallows
1 cup of whipped cream or whipped topping
1/2 cup of Greek yogurt (plain or vanilla flavored)
1 tablespoon of honey (optional, adjust to taste)
1/4 cup of shredded coconut (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

Prepare the Fruits: Wash and chop the fruits into bite-sized pieces if they are not already prepared.
Mix Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped fruits and miniature marshmallows.
Prepare Dressing: In a separate bowl, mix together the whipped cream or whipped topping with
Greek yogurt. If you prefer a sweeter salad, you can add honey to the dressing and adjust the
sweetness to your taste.

Ingredients:

1 ripe avocado
2 slices of bread (your choice, but sourdough or whole grain work well)
Salt and pepper to taste
Optional toppings: sliced tomatoes, red pepper flakes, sesame seeds, poached egg, feta cheese, etc.

Instructions:

Start by toasting your bread to your desired level of crispiness.
While the bread is toasting, cut the avocado in half and remove the pit. Scoop the avocado flesh
into a bowl and mash it with a fork until it reaches your desired consistency.
Once the bread is toasted, spread the mashed avocado evenly onto each slice.
Sprinkle salt and pepper over the avocado to taste.
Now, it's time to add your optional toppings. Get creative and add whatever you like! Some
popular choices include sliced tomatoes, red pepper flakes, sesame seeds, a poached egg, or
crumbled feta cheese.

Ingredients:

1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
1 ripe banana
1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
1/2 cup milk (you can use any kind you prefer, such as almond milk,
soy milk, or regular dairy milk)
1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional, depending on how sweet you want it)
Ice cubes (optional, if you want a colder shake)br
Instructions:

Start by rinsing your blueberries if they're fresh.
Peel the banana and break it into smaller chunks.
In a blender, combine the blueberries, banana chunks, Greek yogurt, milk, and
honey or maple syrup if using.
If you want a colder shake, you can add a handful of ice cubes to the blender as well.
Blend all the ingredients until smooth and creamy.

Ingredients:

Sub rolls or bread of your choice
Lettuce
Tomatoes, sliced
Cucumbers, sliced
Bell peppers, sliced
Red onions, sliced
Olives (black or green), sliced
Pickles, sliced
Avocado, sliced (optional)
Cheese slices (optional, if you're not vegan)
Salt and pepper to taste
Condiments of your choice (mayonnaise, mustard, ketchup, etc.)
Seasonings like oregano, basil, or Italian seasoning (optional)
Instructions:

Start by slicing the sub rolls horizontally, but not all the way through, to
create a pocket for the fillings.
If you like your bread toasted, you can lightly toast the rolls in the oven or toaster.
Spread your desired condiments inside the bread, such as mayonnaise, mustard, or any
other sauce you prefer.
Layer the veggies inside the sandwich. You can start with lettuce as the base, followed by tomatoes,
cucumbers, bell peppers, onions, olives, pickles, and avocado slices if using.
If you're adding cheese, place a slice or two on top of the veggies.
Season with salt, pepper, and any other desired seasonings like oregano or basil.
Close the sandwich and press down gently to help the flavors meld together.
You can serve the sandwich immediately or wrap it in parchment paper or foil for later.

Cooking Rice:

Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch.
In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the rinsed rice, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook
until the rice is tender and water is absorbed, about 15-20 minutes. Once done, fluff the rice with
a fork and set it aside to cool slightly.

Tempering:

Heat oil in a large skillet or pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
Now add green chilies, peanuts or cashews (if using), and curry leaves. Saute until the nuts turn
light golden brown.

Mixing:

Add the cooked rice to the skillet. Gently mix everything together, ensuring the rice is coated
evenly with the spices. Be careful not to break the rice grains.

Adding Lemon Juice:

Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the rice. Start with 1 tablespoon and adjust according to your taste
preferences. Mix well.

Final Touch:
Season with salt to taste. Mix again and cook for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Ingredients:

8 ounces (about 225 grams) of spaghetti pasta
2 tablespoons of olive oil
3 cloves of garlic, minced
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes
1 teaspoon of dried basil
1 teaspoon of dried oregano
Salt and pepper to taste
Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)
Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

Cook the spaghetti pasta according to the package instructions in a large pot of boiling salted
water until al dente. Drain the pasta and set aside.
In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion,
and sauté until softened and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
Stir in the diced tomatoes, dried basil, and dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
Let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened.
Add the cooked spaghetti pasta to the skillet with the tomato sauce. Toss well to coat the pasta
evenly with the sauce.
Serve the spaghetti pasta hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves if desired.

Ingredients:

1 cup all-purpose flour
2 tablespoons sugar
1 tablespoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 cup milk
2 tablespoons melted butter (or vegetable oil)
1 large egg
Optional: 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
In another bowl, whisk together the milk, melted butter (or oil), egg, and vanilla extract (if using).
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. It's okay if the
batter is a little lumpy; overmixing can result in tough pancakes.
Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. You can lightly grease the surface with butter
or cooking spray if needed.
Once the skillet is hot, pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until
bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2-3 minutes.
Carefully flip the pancakes with a spatula and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden
brown on the bottom.
Transfer the cooked pancakes to a plate and repeat with the remaining batter. You can keep the
pancakes warm in a low oven while you cook the rest.
Serve the pancakes warm with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, fresh fruit, whipped
cream, or chocolate chips.

Ingredients:

Paneer (Indian cottage cheese), crumbled or sliced
Bread slices (whole wheat or white)
Butter or ghee
Onion, thinly sliced
Tomato, thinly sliced
Green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Chaat masala (optional)
Salt to taste
Pepper to taste

Instructions:

Prepare the Paneer: If the paneer is not already crumbled or sliced, prepare it accordingly. You can
crumble it with your hands or cut it into thin slices.
Prepare the Bread: Take two slices of bread for each sandwich. You can use any type of bread you
prefer, whether it's white, whole wheat, or multigrain.
Layer the Sandwich:

Spread butter or ghee evenly on one side of each bread slice.
Place a few slices or a generous amount of crumbled paneer on one slice of bread.
Top the paneer with thinly sliced onion, tomato, and green chilies (if using).
Sprinkle some chopped coriander leaves over the vegetables.
Optionally, sprinkle a pinch of chaat masala for added flavor.
Season with salt and pepper according to your taste.
Assemble the Sandwich: Place the other slice of bread on top of the paneer and vegetable layer, with
the buttered side facing down.

Cook the Sandwich: You have a few options for cooking the sandwich:

Grill : Preheat a sandwich maker or grill pan. Place the assembled sandwich on the grill and cook
until the bread turns golden brown and crispy, and the paneer filling is heated through.
Pan Fry: Heat a skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Place the assembled sandwich in the pan and
cook on both sides until golden brown and crispy, pressing gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking.

Ingredients:

For the filling:

2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, cabbage, bell peppers, beans, peas, etc.), finely chopped
1 onion, finely chopped
2-3 cloves garlic, minced
1 tablespoon ginger, minced
1 green chili, finely chopped (optional, adjust to taste)
1 teaspoon soy sauce
1 teaspoon vinegar
1 teaspoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil

For the dough:

1 cup all-purpose flour
Water as needed
Salt to taste
Oil for frying

Instructions:

Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chili. Sauté until
onions turn translucent.
Add chopped vegetables (carrots, cabbage, bell peppers, beans, peas, etc.). Stir fry for 5-7 minutes
until the vegetables are cooked but still crisp.
Add soy sauce, vinegar, chili sauce, and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
Remove from heat and let the filling cool down.
Place a spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the sides over
the filling and roll tightly to form a cylinder-shaped roll. Seal the edges using a little water.
